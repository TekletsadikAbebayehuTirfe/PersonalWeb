Biography

I was born on the fourth of February 2000 G.C in Zewditu Hospital. At that time my two
brothers namely Misikir and Habtamu were 9 and 7 years old respectively.Having them
around made my upcoming a lot easier since having an elderly makes you more alert than
those that do not since we tend to gather information easily from them instead of looking
somewhere else. The strangest thing about my growth was the fact that, unlike most children,
I was not able to walk until the age of 1. Back then the neighbour's children started walking before
the age of 1 so my dad used to be mad at me (so I was told). Then on my first year's birthday, the strangest
thing happened; I stood on my own two feet and started walking only fo fall from the stairs. That was my
first major accident during childhood, of course among the others. So that's how my earliest days went by.

At the age of 3, I joined Addis Alem Kindergarten, a school near my house; that made my transport easier
but above all my mom's worries since she feared that sending a kid to a school far away causes problems.
I attended my school there for 2 years before graduating and joining the first grade. I did not learn KG's 3
because I skipped it since I was the top of my class. But later I found that skipping classes is not a good
practice because you don't know what you might be missing. I was not shy at all duringmy early days I even
presented a poem on my graduation day, infront of the whole class; that was the first time that I made my mom
proud, I remember her telling this to every guest who came to our house during the after party. After that, a
new life in Yemane Birhane Primary and Secondary School began.

I joined Yemane Birhane in 1999 E.C; I was quiet during the first 2 weeks or so until I made some friends,
but afterwards, you know how kids are; I was all over the place running around and having lots of fun. On
the first semester I was ranked fifth out of 55 students, I guessed that made me mad a bit since I wasn't used
to being beaten. On the second semester, however, I got the second rank; above all people including my mom
I was the one that was the happiest. So for the other 3 years I was ther I ended all semesters being the top of
my class until I was moved to Akaki Kale Heywet Church School where I remained top for other 5 years.

It was on fifth grade that I joined Kale Heywet, I wasn't quiet as I was in Yemane Birhane since I found
some kids that I know from our village. The education system and the teacher's quality was more robust
than Yemane Birhane may be that was the reason that my family decided to switch schools. There was
somewhat strange but competetive rule in Kale Heywet and it was that if you become a top of your class
for 3 successive semesters, then your education fee for one year will be covered by the school. That created
a real compitition among fellow students, and that also made me more alert and careful not to have my
place taken. 5 Years go by like this and I finished tenth grade scoring with a score of 4.0 and move on to
New English Private School to attend my preparatory education.

New English Private School short for NEPS was a different kind of school for me compared to the other
two. Lots of student from abroad attended there; for instance: England, Nigeria, USA, Jamaica and so on.
I guess now that I think about it, it helped me improve my language skills. But as much as I can I tried to
stay Ethiopian by only speaking amharic unless it's very demanding. The school tried to follow a different
type of curriculum than other preparatory schools but it did not work out well for them; but they had incredible
teachers whom I have learnt so many things from, and out of all teachers, one stands out, Yonas Kibret.
He was my physics teacher and the author of the book "Alpha Physics". His teaching techniques were quite
outstanding and his advies were so constructive and empowering. He also made me want to teach someday.
Two years passed like this and I took the enterance exam and score 536 and join Addis Ababa University.

Another chapter began here in AAU, new friends, new curriculum and so on. I admit I got a bit intimidated
by joining AAU, and this led my grades to be lower. And later that year my father had cancer, that was the
most difficult times of my life because all he and I ever wanted was to see me on that stage wearing that
graduation gown. He died in August 30 2011 E.C. I was devastated that I finished this first semester with an
extremely low grade. But now that I've realized it I should honor him by being the person he wanted me to
be so step by step I'm trying to become that person and the help of my friends and family in doing so is priceless.

As I mentioned earlier, I have two brothers Misikir Ketema and Habtamu Ketema they are the stepping stones
for where I am now by sacrficing their time and energy so that I could be better. Misikir is a health officer in
Betehl Hospital while Habtamu works in Ethio Air Lines. The other pillars for me are my friends who I see as
my brothers from another mother, Sintayehu, Dawit, Amanuel, Abrham, Yeabsira, and Samuel who always strive
to make me a better person than I am today. I am truly grateful for having them in my life. So that was the slight
glimpse of my life summed up.
